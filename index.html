What is ATLAS?

Think of it as a brain for your school district.

Right now, student grades live in one system, attendance in another, IEPs in a third, and test scores in a fourth. Nobody can see the full picture — so problems go unnoticed and interventions come too late.

ATLAS connects all of these systems into one unified view. It uses the same kinds of algorithms that power logistics at Amazon and player analytics in professional sports — except applied to the thing that matters most: student success.

The system never makes decisions on its own. It surfaces insights, proposes evidence-backed actions, and lets educators decide. Every recommendation is traceable, explainable, and grounded in real data.

Core Principles

What ATLAS will never do

🚫

Never Rank Students

ATLAS identifies needs and matches resources. It never produces student rankings, scores for comparison, or labels that follow students across contexts.

🤝

Never Replace Teachers

Every recommendation requires human approval. ATLAS proposes — educators decide. The system augments professional judgment, never overrides it.

🔒

Never Sell Data

Student data belongs to students and their districts. Period. No advertising, no third-party sales, no secondary uses. Privacy is structural, not a policy.
