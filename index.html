What is ATLAS?

Think of it as a brain for your school district.

Right now, a counselor asking "Is this student at risk?" has to manually check six different systems. Student grades live in one place, attendance in another, IEPs in a third, test scores in a fourth. Nobody sees the full picture — so problems go unnoticed and help arrives too late.

ATLAS connects every system into a unified knowledge graph, then reasons about the connections using the same algorithmic approaches that transformed logistics, finance, and professional sports — except applied to what matters most: student success.

The system never acts on its own. It surfaces insights, proposes evidence-backed actions, and waits for an educator to decide. Every recommendation is traceable, explainable, and grounded in real data.

Core Principles

What ATLAS will never do

Trust isn't communicated — trust is structural. These principles are enforced by architecture, not policy.

🚫

Never Rank Students

ATLAS identifies needs and matches resources. It never produces student rankings, comparison scores, or labels that follow students across contexts. Every insight exists to help — never to sort.

🤝

Never Replace Educators

Every recommendation requires human approval. ATLAS proposes — educators decide. The system augments professional judgment with data; it never overrides it. Humans are always in the loop.

🔒

Never Sell Data

Student data belongs to students and their districts. Period. No advertising, no third-party sales, no secondary uses. Privacy is structural: per-district encryption keys, crypto-shredding, and transparent audit logs.
