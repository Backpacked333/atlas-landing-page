Domain Map

Nine operational domains

Each domain is a facet of educational operations. The connections between domains are where ATLAS creates value no point solution can replicate.

Domain 1 The Learner

The gravitational center of the entire ontology. Every other domain exists in service of the learner's development. Not a row in a database — a rich, evolving, multi-dimensional representation.

Core Entities

Learner — Identity, enrollment, program associations, external ID mappings for entity resolution across systems.

LearnerAcademicProfile — Domain mastery map decomposed across every academic subdomain. Not a single “reading level” but phonemic awareness, decoding, fluency, vocabulary, comprehension.

LearnerBehavioralProfile — SEL assessments (DESSA, SAEBRS), behavior event history, engagement patterns.

LearnerWellnessProfile — Attendance patterns (not just present/absent), circumstantial factors (homelessness, foster care), with strict access controls.

LearnerSupportPlan — Universal abstraction for IEPs, 504 plans, intervention plans, gifted plans, EL plans, and private school learning agreements.

Key Relationships

Learner → ENROLLED_AT → School (temporal) Learner → GOVERNED_BY → SupportPlan (zero to many) Learner → ASSESSED_BY → AssessmentEvent (one to many) Learner → RECEIVES_SERVICE → ServiceDelivery Learner → ASSIGNED_TO → Staff (advisor, counselor, case manager)
Domain 2 Instructional Program

What is being taught, according to what framework, using what materials, assessed how, and with what expected outcomes. Framework-agnostic — supports Common Core, IB, Montessori, Waldorf, Classical, and custom frameworks.

Core Entities

CurricularFramework — Hierarchical decomposition into domains, strands, standards, benchmarks. Follows CASE 1.1 for interoperability.

Course / CourseSection — What’s being taught, by whom, when, where, with what materials.

AssessmentInstrument / AssessmentEvent — Every formal and informal assessment with scoring models, psychometric properties, and subdomain disaggregation.

Domain 3 People & Capacities

Every human in the system — their roles, credentials, availability, effectiveness, and workload. A single person may occupy multiple roles.

Staff — Certifications, competencies, workload tracking (max vs. current hours/caseload), availability patterns, multi-school assignments for itinerant providers.

Guardian — Contact info, language preferences, consent records, engagement history.

ExternalProvider — Contracted therapists, tutoring agencies, community partners with credentials and availability.

Domains 4–6 Time, Space & Finance

Time & Scheduling (Domain 4)

Academic calendars, bell schedules, master schedules, schedule blocks, intervention windows. Time is the most constrained resource — every recommendation is gated by temporal constraints.

Space & Resources (Domain 5)

Facilities, rooms (with capacity, equipment, suitability), transportation assets with route optimization, technology resources, material inventories.

Finance & Economics (Domain 6)

Funding sources (with restrictions and clawback risk), budgets, expenditures, cost models (Ingredients Method), and revenue recovery tracking. The “Moneyball” layer.

Domains 7–9 Governance, Relationships & Interventions

Governance & Compliance (Domain 7)

Machine-evaluable constraints from federal law (IDEA, 504, FERPA), state regulations, union contracts, board policies, accreditation standards. Each constraint has severity, scope, and enforcement mechanism.

Relationships & Communication (Domain 8)

Formal relationships (teacher-student, advisor-advisee), communication events across all channels, community partners with referral processes and capacity.

Interventions & Support Systems (Domain 9)

Intervention catalog with evidence tiers, assignment workflow (12-state machine), service delivery tracking, progress monitoring, and the complete intervention lifecycle from identification through adaptation.

Reasoning Patterns

Four universal patterns of thought

Every feature ATLAS builds is an instantiation of one or more of these patterns.

🚨

Pattern 1: Signal → Root Cause

Something changes in the data. The system detects it, evaluates significance, and traces causal pathways across domains to identify what is actually going on.

🎯

Pattern 2: Need → Resource Match

Given an identified need, enumerate all resources that could address it, evaluate against all constraints, and propose the optimal match with tradeoff analysis.

🔄

Pattern 3: Act → Monitor → Adapt

Once approved, orchestrate execution, monitor fidelity and outcomes against projections, and trigger adaptation when reality diverges from expectations.

📈

Pattern 4: Optimize → Maximize Impact

Given finite resources (people, time, money, space), determine the allocation that maximizes aggregate educational outcomes, weighted by equity.

Auditability

Decision provenance

Every recommendation is a reified subgraph — a first-class, inspectable, auditable object. Any stakeholder can see exactly why a recommendation was made.

What a provenance chain captures

1
Trigger — What data point or event started the analysis
2
Traversal path — Every node visited, every relationship followed, every data point inspected
3
Constraints evaluated — Which rules were checked, which passed, which failed
4
Alternatives considered — What other options were evaluated and why they were not selected
5
Conclusion — The recommendation with confidence score and human-readable reasoning
6
Review trail — Who approved, when, and any modifications or disputes

Adaptability

One ontology, any context

The same structure serves a 200-student Montessori school and an 80,000-student urban district. Variance is in configuration, not architecture.

🏫

Small Private School

200 students, 30 staff. Non-standard frameworks (Montessori, Waldorf). Accreditation standards replace legal mandates. Same ontology, different constraint content.

🏙

Large Urban District

60K students, 4K staff, 80 schools. Full federal/state regulatory framework. Complex union contracts. Itinerant staff routing across dozens of sites.

👥

Charter Network

15 schools, 8K students. Hybrid context: public funding with operational flexibility. Network-level optimization across schools.
