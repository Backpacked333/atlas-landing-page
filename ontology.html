Semantic Ontology

Everything connects to the student

Nine operational domains, hundreds of entities, thousands of relationships — all centered on the learner. The ontology isn’t a database schema. It’s a reasoning substrate where every traversal reveals insights no single system could produce alone.

Complete entity catalog — Instructional domain

CurricularFramework

Hierarchical decomposition into domains, strands, standards, benchmarks. Follows CASE 1.1 for interoperability. Supports Common Core, IB, Montessori, Waldorf, Classical, and custom frameworks.

Course / CourseSection

What’s being taught, by whom, when, where, with what materials. CourseSection is the operational unit — a specific teacher teaching a specific course in a specific room at a specific time.

AssessmentInstrument / AssessmentEvent

Every formal and informal assessment with scoring models, psychometric properties, and subdomain disaggregation. AssessmentEvent captures a single administration with accommodations, validity, and raw + scaled scores.

Staff

Temporal modeling — bitemporal everything

ATLAS maintains two time dimensions for every fact using XTDB v2:

Valid-time: When a fact was true in the real world (e.g., student enrolled on Sept 1).

System-time: When the system learned about it (e.g., data ingested on Sept 3).

This enables “as-of” queries: “What did the system know about this student on October 15?” — critical for FERPA compliance, audit trails, and understanding how decisions were made with the information available at the time.

-- XTDB temporal query: what was true at a specific moment SELECT * FROM learner_enrollment FOR VALID_TIME AS OF DATE '2025-10-15' FOR SYSTEM_TIME AS OF TIMESTAMP '2025-10-16T09:00:00Z' WHERE learner_id = 'abc123'
